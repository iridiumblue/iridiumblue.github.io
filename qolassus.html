Qolassus

A plagiarist's worst nightmare.

Qolassus scans the web for other people using your Quora content. Whether you're quoted with attribution in the New York Times or some hack blogger is just jacking your stuff - Qolassus will scour the web for all your answers and provide a full report.

To get your Qolassus report,

  1. Download your full archive of answers using quArk. (Firefox only.)
  2. Email the Quora_Archive file to me, christopher.j.reiss@gmail.com
  3. Pay me $20 (or something). I'll reply and let you know when I'll be emailing your Qolassus report (probably a day or two.)

Why a fee? I originally planned to make Qolassus a free plugin like Quark. That turned out to be impossible. Search engines don't like to be hammered with thousands of search requests in a row. So I had to implement certain, um, work-arounds. These workarounds require a rather exotic environment (access to a hundred or so proxy servers), and some personal attention while they churn. This is also the reason that I can only process a few of these per day.

That said, I don't want to deny anyone who can't afford it. If $20 is too steep, $10 or $5 is fine too. And if you can't spare a dime, just let me know and I'll be happy to provide it for free.

Personally, when I ran this thing on my own content (about 3,000 answers), I was fairly startled as I scrolled through over a hundred incidents of my stuff ending up in strange locations.

A good many were legit : Media outlets like Business Insider - and smaller publishers - giving full credit. Or random websites that also did the right thing and attributed the text.

But most of it was just plagiarism. And man, quite a vigorous assortment ! Here's a sample of what Qollasus turned up :

  • I ended up on some dude's motivational poster in Pakistan.
  • I made it into a motor scooter manual! (Kind of proud of that one.)
  • Some chick on a professional forum just pasted an answer of mine in a discussion thread. Other people applauded her writing style. I took a smug, secret bow.
  • I ended up in lots of places I would never have though to look. Pinterest. Tumbler. That sort of thing

I can't say I'm mad about it all. Mostly flattered, I guess.

Will I do anything about it? Nah. Well - I may log into that professional forum, email me if you can think of something really funny to say!
